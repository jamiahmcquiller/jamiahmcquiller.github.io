ASSESSING AFFECT AND ENGAGEMENT IN HUMAN ROBOT TEAMING

I provided valuable assistance to Associate Professor of Computer Science Mark Allison in his significant role within a large NSF-funded research project focused on teamwork and robotics. Collaborating closely with Professor Allison, I actively contributed to the analysis of cutting-edge software designed to discern human emotions using an impressive array of state-of-the-art data collection tools. These tools encompassed the Emotiv EEG headset, the Facial Action Coding System, the Brain-Computer Interface, and OpenFace. My role involved a comprehensive investigation into how these advanced data collection tools could enable robots to adapt to the ever-changing emotional and stress-related fluctuations in human behavior. Specifically, I employed OpenFace's affect recognition software to identify the six fundamental emotions (anger, sadness, surprise, fear, disgust, and happiness). While these emotions generally manifest themselves in facial muscles in a fairly consistent manner, it's important to note that certain factors like deception, individual emotional baselines, or cultural distinctions could introduce variations. To address this, Professor Allison and I complemented our facial analysis with EEG readings obtained from an Emotiv Insight headset. Upon concluding our analysis, we made a significant discovery: the combined use of the Facial Action Coding System (FACS) and Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) could effectively capture and reflect emotional states. This breakthrough represents a crucial advancement in our ongoing efforts to enhance the collaboration and performance of teams composed of both robots and humans.

4D Flow Cardiac MRI Data Visualization using Virtual Reality (VR) Technology

Zillion Solutions is an esteemed campus-wide competition that beckons graduate, undergraduate students from all disciplines, including undecided scholars, dual enrolled individuals, and even Genesee County high school students, to unleash their creativity by crafting either a concise video or a compelling slide deck outlining their groundbreaking concepts for novel products or services aimed at solving real-world problems. In this dynamic arena of innovation, I proudly showcased my visionary project centered around harnessing the power of Virtual Reality (VR) technology to revolutionize the visualization of 4D Flow Cardiac MRI Data. The realm of 4D flow cardiac magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) stands as a pinnacle in modern imaging techniques, furnishing unparalleled insights into the intricate workings of heart chambers and arterial networks with unprecedented detail. With each advancement in 4D flow MRI, the horizon of cardiovascular disease evaluation broadens, offering clinicians an unmatched arsenal for conducting comprehensive quantitative assessments of blood flows within the cardiovascular system. Yet, in the quest for excellence, I recognized a potential avenue for further enhancement. The effectiveness of 4D flow MRI, while formidable on its own, could soar to unprecedented heights through integration with real-time immersive visualization technologies, particularly Virtual Reality (VR) systems. Through diligent research and innovation, I embarked on the journey of creating a bespoke VR environment meticulously tailored to seamlessly integrate 4D flow MRI scans with cutting-edge wireless VR systems. This innovative synergy not only empowers medical professionals to interact with the scanned data in real-time but also facilitates the extraction of quantitative features, thereby amplifying the precision of cardiovascular diagnostics to unprecedented levels. This project propelled me from the ranks of semi-finalists to the coveted position of a finalist, eventually clinching the prestigious title of 1st Runner-up amidst fierce competition, with a staggering 863 submissions vying for recognition. It stands as a testament to the transformative potential of merging technology with healthcare, underscoring the profound impact that innovation can wield in reshaping the landscape of medical diagnostics and treatment.

Revolutionizing Waste Management with IoT

Jamiah McQuiller and Marwa Hammami's project, "Automatic Trash Can," utilizes Arduino IoT technology, an Ultrasonic Sensor, and a Servo Motor to create a hands-free waste disposal system. The system detects motion within 1" to 13 feet using infrared technology, triggering the lid to open automatically. The project aims to simplify waste management tasks and reduce germ transmission by eliminating the need for physical contact with the trash can. Key Components and Working Principle: - Ultrasonic Sensor: Detects objects, such as human hands, triggering the Servo Motor. - Servo Motor: Opens the lid of the trash can upon motion detection. - Arduino IoT Carrier: Utilizes a Passive Infrared Sensor (PIR) to detect motion and exchange data over the Internet. - Alarm System: Alerts users of detected motion through a message display and blinking red light. Customization and Control: - Potentiometers: Adjust the detection distance and reaction time of the PIR sensor. - Arduino IoT Cloud Dashboard: Allows users to monitor and control the system remotely. Benefits and Applications: - Streamlines waste disposal tasks and minimizes waste generation. - Reduces germ transmission by eliminating physical contact with the trash can. - Suitable for various environments such as homes, offices, hospitals, etc. - Can be modified and expanded to meet specific needs and preferences. Overall, the project demonstrates how technology can enhance daily routines, improve environmental sustainability, and promote better health practices through innovative and practical solutions. It serves as an accessible and adaptable project for both novice and experienced hobbyists.
