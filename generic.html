Consider Me Represented

Consider Me Represented

I'm an African American woman from a low-income background who has faced educational challenges ranging from limited access to quality resources, insufficient funding for her local schools, and other systemic barriers. As I am pursuing Cybersecurity and navigating the tech industry, I have faced racial, gender, and socioeconomic barriers in a predominantly white male-dominated space including absence of representation, implicit biases, and unequal pathways for career growth.

As a single mom, I bring a unique perspective shaped by the challenges and triumphs of balancing career aspirations with the responsibilities of raising a family solo. This experience has honed my resilience, assertiveness, and perseverance - qualities that are invaluable in the fast-paced world of technology. I am driven not only by my passion for innovation but also by the desire to create a better future for my child and inspire others facing similar circumstances.

I’m conducting research with Yasser Aboelkassem to revolutionize cardiac MRI with 4D flow imaging, leveraging Virtual Reality for immersive visualization. By integrating advanced technology, I’m enhancing diagnostics, minimizing risks, and shaping the future of noninvasive surgery. I've been studying cybersecurity and computer science at the College of Innovation and Technology and am driven to bridge medicine and technology. Before this, I collaborated with Mark Allison and other students on his NSF-funded research project looking at teamwork and robotics. Toward that end, I helped with various forms of Autoencoders that can combine to translate humans' emotional states into data that robots can respond to so we can program them to adjust to the emotional and fatigue states of the humans around them.

I am engaging with this research because I believe that representation matters, especially in tech. I aim to break barriers and amplify diverse voices - cultivate an inclusive tech ecosystem that values and empowers individuals from all backgrounds. Through advocacy, mentorship, and innovation, I’m committed to forging a path where everyone has the opportunity to thrive and contribute to the future of technology.

Consider me represented.
